## Critical Compliance Requirements

### AVOID These Claims
❌ Weight loss outcomes
❌ Anti-aging promises
❌ Guaranteed results
❌ Comparative statements implying superiority to FDA-approved drugs
❌ Suggesting prescribers use compounded products when FDA-approved is medically appropriate
❌ Advertising or promoting medications as cures

### FORBIDDEN Language
❌ "Miracle"
❌ "Cure"
❌ "Guaranteed"
❌ "FDA-approved" (for compounded medications)

### Required Compliance Statements

**Statement 1 (Prescription Acknowledgment)**:
"By submitting a prescription or order request to Mythic Pharmacy, you acknowledge that all compounded medications are prepared pursuant to a valid patient-specific prescription, are not FDA-approved, and must be dispensed and used under the supervision of a licensed prescriber. You understand that Mythic Pharmacy does not provide medical diagnosis, treatment decisions, or telehealth services."

**Statement 2 (Licensing)**:
"Mythic Pharmacy can only dispense medications in states where we hold an active pharmacy license. We are currently licensed in: Utah, Idaho, Arizona, Colorado, Wyoming, and Hawaii. Additional states will be added as licenses are approved."

## Target Audience Segments

### Primary Audience
Licensed healthcare providers or clinics seeking reliable, science-forward compounding partner

### Specific Segments
1. **Weight loss and metabolic clinics** - seeking consistent GLP compounding pharmacy
2. **Aesthetic and wellness clinics** - offering injections, peptides, or hormone therapy
3. **Primary care practices** - seeking personalized medications for chronic care patients
4. **Functional medicine practices** - seeking advanced custom formulations
5. **Hormone clinics**

### Geographic Target
Utah, Idaho, Arizona, Colorado, Wyoming, Hawaii, Nevada

### Top Motivations
- Fast and reliable turnaround times
- Knowledgeable pharmacists who collaborate with their clinical team
- Pharmacy that cares about patient experience as much as clinical outcomes
- "Apothecary meets science" aesthetic

### Common Barriers
- Past bad experiences with inconsistent compounding pharmacies
- Concerns about supply shortages
- Liability concerns around compounded alternatives vs. FDA-approved drugs
- Not understanding quality standards

## Marketing Copy Guidelines

### DO:
✅ Emphasize safety, quality, precision
✅ Highlight collaboration with clinical teams
✅ Use warm but professional language
✅ Be transparent about processes
✅ Focus on provider partnership
✅ Mention custom formulation capabilities
✅ Discuss turnaround times and reliability
✅ Explain quality standards clearly

### DON'T:
❌ Make direct-to-consumer appeals
❌ Promise specific health outcomes
❌ Use superlative claims without evidence
❌ Suggest compounded medications replace FDA-approved options
❌ Use prohibited language (miracle, cure, guaranteed, etc.)
❌ Target patients directly (only providers)

## Current Website Issues to Address

### Generic Template Language
The current website uses generic pharmacy/healthcare language:
- "Stay Healthy with Our Quality Medical Products"
- "Healthcare Solutions for a Healthier Tomorrow"
- Generic mission/vision statements
- Consumer-focused language
- No mention of compounding specifics
- Missing provider-centric messaging

### Needed Changes
1. Replace consumer language with provider-focused messaging
2. Remove generic "medical products" references
3. Add compounding-specific terminology
4. Emphasize B2B partnership model
5. Include compliance statements
6. Add trust badges and quality certifications
7. Focus on custom formulation capabilities
8. Highlight clinic collaboration model
